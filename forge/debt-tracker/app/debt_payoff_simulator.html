Read before acting

How currency conversion works

Each debt is held in its own currency. The dashboard converts every balance into the quote currency using the static rates in rates.js, so the payoff maths can compare them. Historical payments are converted at today’s rate, not the rate on the day you paid — fine for planning, not for exact accounting.

Credit cards work differently to loans

A card’s minimum is a percentage of the balance (with a floor), so it shrinks as you pay down — which is why minimum-only payments drag on for years. The avalanche still attacks the highest rate first; cards usually sit near the top.

Money from friends

Modelled at their stated rate (usually 0%). With 0% and no set minimum, the maths ranks them last — but the relationship may not. Set payFirst: true on a friend in debts.js to repay them ahead of everything else.

Balances are estimates; minimums are assumed paid

The roll-forward assumes you pay each minimum in full every month. Update debts.js with a real balance (and a new snapshot date) whenever you have one. When a debt clears, log the freed-up payment as an extra to the next target so the tracker stays accurate.
